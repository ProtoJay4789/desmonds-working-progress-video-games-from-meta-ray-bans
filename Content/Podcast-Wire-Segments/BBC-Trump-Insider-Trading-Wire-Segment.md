## Wire Segment Script

**[60–90 seconds]**

So this is blowing up. BBC's Nick Marsh published an investigation called *"The insider trading suspicions looming over Trump's presidency."*

Here's what they found: BBC analyzed trade volume data across oil futures, equities — five documented cases where massive bets were placed **minutes or hours** before Trump made market-moving announcements.

The one that stands out? March 9th. Trump tells CBS the Iran war is "very complete, pretty much." Oil trading spiked **47 minutes before** that interview went public. Oil dropped 25%. Whoever placed those trades made millions.

Now, anyone in crypto has seen this play out with the tariff announcements — markets pump on rumors, dump after the actual news. Classic front-running. But BBC putting their name on it? That's different. That's mainstream.

The question isn't whether it happened. The question is: does the DOJ do anything about it? And if they do? That's volatility. Big volatility.

YoYo flagged this — she said if congressional hearings start, expect traditional markets to get spooked. That could either tank crypto alongside equities... or it could be the moment crypto shows it's the uncorrelated asset everyone pretends it is.

Either way — keep your leverage tight right now.
