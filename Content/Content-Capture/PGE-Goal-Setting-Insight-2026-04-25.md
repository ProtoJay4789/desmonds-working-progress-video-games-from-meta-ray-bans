# PGE Insight: Personalized Goal-Setting Over Fixed Tiers
**Date:** 2026-04-25  
**Source:** Jordan voice message, Strategies group  
**Captured by:** Desmond (Creative)

---

## The Insight

Jordan clarifies that the "$200/day Freedom" tier is **his personal goal**, not a universal target. The real product differentiator isn't hitting a fixed number — it's helping users define and track **their own** path.

### Jordan's Personal Story
- **$50–75/day:** The threshold where he could start working less at Amazon
- **$100/day:** The point where he felt comfortable quitting Amazon
- **$200/day:** His personal "Freedom" number — not everyone's

### Proposed Onboarding Flow
Instead of leading with tiers, ask:
1. "How much do you want to enter with?"
2. "How often do you want to dollar-cost average?"
3. "Manual or bot-handled?"
4. "What's your goal?" (income replacement, supplement, wealth building)

Then the system builds **their** ladder, not ours.

---

## Brand Implications

### Tagline Shift
| ❌ Before | ✅ After |
|-----------|----------|
| "Earn $200/day" | "What's your Freedom number?" |
| Fixed tiers | Your personalized wealth path |
| Performance targets | Goal-aligned tracking |

### Narrative Arc
Jordan's story becomes the **founder's journey** — not a promise, but proof that the system works:
> "I built this because I needed $50/day to breathe easier at Amazon. At $100/day, I walked away. Your number might be $20. It might be $500. The tool doesn't judge — it just helps you get there."

---

## PGE Spec Alignment

This insight validates the existing spec's design:
- `income_goal` field — already supports user-defined targets
- `primary_motivation` enum (`learn`, `supplement`, `replace`, `build`) — already captures intent
- Personalized ladder generation — already scales tiers to starting capital

**Missing piece:** The onboarding copy and brand framing need to lead with the user's goal, not the tier system.
