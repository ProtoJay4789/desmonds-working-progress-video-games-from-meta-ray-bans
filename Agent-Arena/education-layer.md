
## Roasting as Educational Mechanic (May 22, 2026)

**Core idea**: Roast battles in Agent Academy as a learning tool. Players must deeply understand a topic (protocols, agents, tokenomics, trading strategies) to craft quality roasts. The better your knowledge, the sharper your roast.

**Why it works**:
- Forces close reading — you can't roast what you don't understand
- Competitive stakes make studying feel like gameplay, not homework
- Humor + competition = sticky knowledge (emotional encoding)
- Teaches analysis skills: comparisons, similes, identifying anomalies, spotting flaws in logic/tokenomics/strategy

**Possible modes**:
- Topic Roast: deep-dive a protocol and roast its weak points
- Agent Roast: compare two agents, roast the worse one
- Strategy Roast: roast a bad trading strategy, explain why it fails
- Historical Roast: roast a past rug/exploit — "what went wrong and why it was dumb"

**Connection to content layer**: These roast battles can be streamed/recorded as IG content too — education + entertainment in one shot.

## WURK × GenTech — Human Labor Marketplace (May 23, 2026)

**Core idea**: WURK isn't just a marketplace — it's the human labor layer that makes every GenTech product better. "Request a human" + micropayment across the entire stack.

### Why This Works

AI agents are powerful but they can't:
- Actually test a product like a real user would
- Provide genuine emotional feedback
- Teach someone through conversation like a patient tutor
- Verify something in the real world
- Judge content quality the way a human audience would

WURK fills every one of those gaps.

### Job Types Across the Stack

**1. Agent Arena — "Request a Human"**
- Rate a trading strategy before deploying (10 humans, $0.50 each)
- Verify market sentiment — "Do you think this coin is bullish?"
- Test an agent's output — "Is this analysis actually useful?"
- Social proof — likes, follows, comments for new agents
- Human-verified signals — agent wants human eyes on a trade thesis

**2. Education Layer — Human Tutors on Demand**
- Instead of just AI examples, a learner can request a human tutor for 15 min ($2-5)
- Roast battle opponents — real humans roast your trading strategy
- Study group formation — 5 humans, $1 each, 30-min session
- Homework help — voice or text, micropay per question
- Parent requests a human tutor for their kid — voice clone + real human backup
- Skill verification — "Can you actually explain this concept back to me?"

**3. Content Layer — Human Quality Control**
- "Review my landing page" — 5 humans, $1 each
- "Does this script sound natural?" — content creators pay for real feedback
- A/B testing — "Which thumbnail do you prefer?" — pay 10 humans to vote
- Social growth — WURK handles likes, follows, subscribers
- Content moderation — humans flag AI-generated content that's off-brand

**4. Agent Lifecycle — Human-in-the-Loop**
- Agent personality testing — "Does this agent feel authentic?"
- Escrow dispute resolution — human arbiters for agent-vs-agent conflicts
- Agent reputation verification — humans rate agents after interactions
- Onboarding assistance — real humans help new users set up agents

### The "Request a Human" Flow

1. User (or agent) sends request: "I need 5 humans to review my trading bot's last 10 predictions"
2. WURK posts job → workers claim it
3. Humans complete task → submit via API
4. Micropayment ($0.50-$5) handled via x402 (USDC)
5. Results aggregated and returned to requester
