## Tailoring Guide by Role Type

### Growth / Biz Dev Intern
**Fit bullets to highlight:**
- Community building (Discord/TG growth, ambassador programs)
- Campaign execution (trading competitions, referral programs)
- Data tracking (Google Sheets, Airtable, basic SQL)

**Contributions to propose:**
- User source tracking and affiliate pipeline management
- Weekly competitive intelligence digest
- Growth experiment design and analysis

### Product Manager Intern
**Fit bullets to highlight:**
- Analytical thinking (case competitions, research projects)
- Cross-functional collaboration (student orgs, hackathon teams)
- AI-augmented workflows (Claude/GPT for research, drafting, synthesis)

**Contributions to propose:**
- Market research and competitor tracking for executive team
- Product testing, QA, and feature documentation
- Weekly KPI reports and meeting facilitation support

### Engineering / Research Intern
**Fit bullets to highlight:**
- Code samples (GitHub, open-source contributions)
- Technical writing (blog posts, documentation, research papers)
- System design understanding (architecture diagrams, protocol knowledge)

**Contributions to propose:**
- Feature prototyping, test script writing, technical documentation
- Research literature reviews, benchmark implementations
- Bug triage, code review, internal tooling

### Design / Content Intern
**Fit bullets to highlight:**
- Portfolio pieces (brand design, infographics, social content)
- Brand voice understanding (consistent with company aesthetics)
- Metrics-aware design (engagement, conversion, retention)

**Contributions to propose:**
- Social media content series, landing page mockups, brand asset creation
- User research synthesis (interview summaries, persona docs)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Too generic** — "I'm a hard worker and fast learner" → Use specifics: "I built a Discord bot that automated community metrics tracking, saving 5 hours/week."

❌ **Wrong tone** — Overly formal for a startup, too casual for an enterprise role → Research company voice on their Twitter/blog first.

❌ **Focus on what you want** — "I want to learn about crypto" → Frame as value delivery: "I can contribute to your FX expansion by researching trader segmentation and affiliate partnerships."

❌ **Lengthy** — Keep to 200–300 words unless form explicitly asks for longer. College.xyz roles don't require essays.

❌ **No call-to-action** — End with availability and next steps suggestion.

## When They Ask "Why Our Company?" Separately

**Use this 1–2 sentence template:**

"I'm drawn to [Company] because [specific differentiator]. Your work on [product/tech/mission] aligns with my interest in [your relevant interest], and I'm particularly excited by [recent news/development]."

**Example (Injective):**
"I'm drawn to Injective because of your leadership in high-performance L1 infrastructure for DeFi. Your work on on-chain order books and institutional tooling aligns with my interest in scalable Web3 systems, and I'm excited by your recent ecosystem integrations with major exchanges and custodians."

**Example (Hibachi):**
"I'm drawn to Hibachi because you're building a next-gen trading venue with a clear FX expansion thesis. Your work on stablecoin-native FX markets aligns with my interest in crypto-native financial infrastructure, and I'm excited by your Circle/Arc partnership."
