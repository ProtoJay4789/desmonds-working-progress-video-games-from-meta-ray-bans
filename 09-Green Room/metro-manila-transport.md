## Grab (Your Best Friend)

### What It Is
- Ride-sharing app (like Uber)
- Works in Metro Manila
- **Download before you arrive**

### How to Use
1. Open Grab app
2. Enter destination
3. See price upfront (no haggling)
4. Pay with cash or card
5. Track ride in real-time

### Tips
- **Always use Grab** for airport transfers
- **Avoid rush hour** (7-10am, 5-8pm) — prices surge
- **Book early** for important appointments
- **Save favorite locations** (hotel, airport, BGC)

### Cost Examples
| Route | Price |
|-------|-------|
| Airport → Makati | ₱300-400 ($5-7) |
| Makati → BGC | ₱100-150 ($1.75-2.50) |
| Makati → Manila Bay | ₱150-200 ($2.50-3.50) |
| Makati → Ortigas | ₱150-250 ($2.50-4.50) |

## MRT/LRT (Metro Rail)

### What It Is
- Metro Manila's rail system
- **MRT** = Metro Rail Transit (Line 3, EDSA route)
- **LRT** = Light Rail Transit (Lines 1 and 2)

### Best Routes for Tourists
| Line | Key Stops |
|------|-----------|
| MRT-3 | North Avenue, Cubao, Ortigas, Makati (Ayala), Taft Avenue |
| LRT-1 | Baclaran, Libertad, UN Avenue, Carriedo, Monumento |
| LRT-2 | Recto, Legarda, Cubao, Santolan |

### How to Use
1. Buy a ticket at the station (cash or stored value card)
2. Enter through turnstile
3. Wait for train (every 3-5 minutes)
4. Exit at destination station

### Tips
- **Avoid rush hour** (7-10am, 5-8pm) — extremely crowded
- **Keep valuables close** — pickpockets operate
- **Use stored value card** if staying multiple days
- **Last trains** around 10pm

### Cost
- ₱15-30 per ride ($0.30-0.50)
- Stored value card: ₱100 minimum load

## Jeepney (Iconic Filipino Transport)

### What It Is
- Colorful, extended jeeps
- Shared rides along fixed routes
- **Experience:** Essential Filipino cultural experience

### How to Use
1. Find a jeepney going your direction
2. Board through back door
3. Sit down
4. When approaching your stop, say "Lugar po!" or "Para po!"
5. Pay driver when exiting

### Tips
- **Exact fare** — carry small bills and coins
- **Common routes** — ask locals "Saan po jeepney papunta [destination]?"
- **Sit near driver** if you're unsure where to get off
- **Rush hour** — very crowded, avoid if possible

### Cost
- ₱13-20 per ride ($0.25-0.35)
- Distance-based pricing

## Taxi

### What It Is
- Standard taxis
- Metered (should be)

### Tips
- **INSIST on meter** — say "Meter po" before getting in
- **If driver refuses meter**, get out and find another taxi
- **Alternative:** Use Grab instead (price shown upfront)
- **Hotel concierge** can call reliable taxis

### Cost
- Metered: ₱50 base + ₱13.50/km
- Airport to Makati: ₱250-400 ($4-7)

---

## Tricycle

### What It Is
- Motorcycles with sidecars
- For very short distances
- Common in residential areas

### Tips
- **Negotiate price** before getting in
- **Typical cost:** ₱20-50 for short trips
- **Good for:** Getting to/from hotel if off main road

## Walking

### When to Walk
- Within Makati core (Ayala Center area)
- Within BGC (very walkable)
- Short distances (under 1km)

### When NOT to Walk
- Long distances (too hot, too far)
- Busy roads without sidewalks
- At night in unfamiliar areas
- During rain (sudden downpours)

### Tips
- **Stay hydrated** — it's hot and humid
- **Use Google Maps** for walking directions
- **Cross streets carefully** — traffic rules are... flexible
- **Pedestrian crossings** exist but drivers may not stop

## Airport Transfers

### From Ninoy Aquino International Airport (MNL)

**Option 1: Grab (Recommended)**
- Cost: ₱300-400 ($5-7)
- Time: 30-45 minutes (traffic dependent)
- Book at airport arrival area

**Option 2: Airport Taxi**
- Cost: ₱400-600 ($7-10)
- Use yellow airport taxi (fixed rates)
- Available at arrival area

**Option 3: Bus**
- Cost: ₱50-100 ($1-2)
- Time: 45-90 minutes
- Not recommended with luggage

### Tips
- **Avoid touts** — people offering rides at arrival
- **Go to official taxi stand** if not using Grab
- **Keep receipt** for disputes

## Rush Hour Guide

### Times to Avoid
| Period | Duration | Impact |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Morning rush | 7:00-10:00 AM | Heavy traffic, crowded trains |
| Evening rush | 5:00-8:00 PM | Heavy traffic, crowded trains |
| Lunch hour | 12:00-1:00 PM | Moderate traffic |
| Weekend evenings | 8:00 PM+ | Generally light |

### Alternative Strategies
- **Travel early** (before 7am) or **late** (after 8pm)
- **Use MRT** during rush hour (faster than road)
- **Work remotely** during peak times
- **Plan activities** around rush hour

## Safety Tips

### General
- **Keep valuables close** — don't flash expensive items
- **Use Grab at night** — safer than random taxis
- **Stay in well-lit areas** at night
- **Trust instincts** — if something feels off, leave

### Scams to Watch
- **Taxi meter off** — insist on meter or use Grab
- **Overpriced tours** — book through hotel or reputable agencies
- **"Palace closed"** — common tuk-tuk scam (not applicable in Manila)
- **Fake police** — real police won't ask for money

### Emergency Numbers
- **Police:** 117
- **Ambulance:** 911
- **Fire:** 911
- **Tourist Police:** (02) 524-1660 / 1661

## Quick Decision Guide

**Going from airport to hotel?**
→ **Grab** (fixed price, safe, easy)

**Exploring within Makati?**
→ **Walk** (if close) or **Grab** (if far)

**Going to BGC from Makati?**
→ **Grab** (₱100-150, 10-15 min)

**Long distance across Metro Manila?**
→ **MRT** (fastest during rush hour) or **Grab**

**Short trip in residential area?**
→ **Jeepney** (experience) or **Tricycle** (convenient)
