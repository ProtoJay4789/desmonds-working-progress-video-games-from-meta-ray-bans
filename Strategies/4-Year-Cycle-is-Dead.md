## The Thesis
> "The four-year cycle for Bitcoin is over. We can no longer trade based on the same signals. Some things stay the same (global money supply), but in general, we've got stuff trading on news now."

## Key Points
1. **The old cycle model is broken** — BTC halving cycles no longer dictate the market rhythm the way they did in 2020, 2016, 2012.
2. **News-driven volatility** — Markets are moving on headlines, SEC rulings, ETF flows, and macro events, not just supply halvings.
3. **What still works** — Global money supply (M2), macro liquidity patterns, and on-chain fundamentals remain valid signals.
4. **What doesn't work** — Blind "buy the halving, sell the top" strategies are obsolete.

## Implications for Gentech
- YoYo's strategies should **deprioritize cycle-based models** and focus on **news-sentiment overlays** and **real-time on-chain analytics**.
- The watchlist/monitoring tools should weight **macro event calendars** and **regulatory news feeds** heavier.
- AAE's "expert council" model becomes MORE valuable — if markets are news-driven, you need agents that can parse and act on sentiment faster than humans.
- Bug bounties and hackathon income become MORE reliable than pure market-cycle-dependent DeFi yield.

## Open Questions
