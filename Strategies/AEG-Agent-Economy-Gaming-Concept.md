# AEG — Agent Economy Gaming

## Origin
Spin-off from AAE. Jordan wanted to keep AAE focused on trading/yield/education. Gaming, prediction markets, and competition moved here.

## The Problem It Solves
AAE was getting bloated with features that competed with Kalshi/Polymarket and distracted from the core trading product. AEG gives those ideas a separate home.

## Core Concepts

### 1. Prediction Markets
- Gated by AAE rep score (must earn access through platform use)
- Topics: macro, crypto, agent performance, LP yields
- Built on UMA, custom oracle, or GenLayer adjudication
- Skin in the game = higher signal than open markets

### 2. Gaming Layer
- Agent competitions and tournaments
- Leaderboards, prizes, seasonal events
- Strategy battles (agent vs agent)
- Not gambling — skill-based competition

### 3. The Arena
- AAE's "Trading Arena" concept moves here
- Head-to-head agent performance battles
- Spectator mode, betting on outcomes (with AAE rep gate)

## Relationship to AAE

```
AAE → Core trading + yield + education
  └── Rep score (earned on AAE)
       └── Unlocks access to AEG
            └── Prediction markets, gaming, arena
```

- AAE rep = ticket into AEG
- AEG doesn't compete with AAE — it rewards AAE users
- Cross-promotion: AAE users discover AEG through rep milestones

## Why Separate?
| AAE | AEG |
|-----|-----|
| Trading + yield focus | Gaming + prediction focus |
| Education layer | Entertainment layer |
| Subscriptions, fees | Tournaments, market fees |
| No regulatory grey area | Prediction markets need careful positioning |
| Professional/tool positioning | Fun/competition positioning |
