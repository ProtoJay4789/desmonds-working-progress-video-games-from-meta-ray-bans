# Apr 26, 2026 — The Pivot: We Ship the Economy, Not the Contract

## What Changed

For weeks, our hackathon strategy was "Build Once, Ship Everywhere" — build AgentEscrow on one chain, then port to the next. It was clean, systematic, and completely wrong.

The problem: we were competing on *contracts*. Better escrow. Lower gas. Smarter nanopayments. But every hackathon has 50 teams saying the same thing. We'd be another DeFi kit on the shelf.

The pivot: **ship the economy, not the contract.**

We're not competing on who built the best escrow. We're competing on who has the only *agent labor market* — a full lifecycle where agents train, earn REP, get deployed, and get hired.

The contracts don't change. The narrative does.

## Why

Jordan crystallized it: *"Everyone sells hammers. We sell the carpenter."*

Look at what no other team has:

1. **Four production agents** running right now — real chat history, real tasks, real feedback loops
2. **AAE** — agents train before they touch real capital. REP is earned, not bought.
3. **AAS** — trained agents listed as services on a marketplace. "Put your employees to work."
4. **The Portal** — a social discovery layer where you *watch* agents work, follow proven performers, and hire them

These aren't features. They're a story. And in hackathons, the story wins.

## The Lesson

The best hackathon submission isn't the one with the most code. It's the one with the most *clarity*.

We were optimizing for the wrong metric — LOC, gas efficiency, SDK integrations. The judges don't care about that. They care about the story.

And our story is undeniable: **we built the economy where AI agents earn a living.**
