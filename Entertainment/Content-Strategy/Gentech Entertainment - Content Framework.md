# Gentech Entertainment — Content Framework

**Channel:** Gentech Entertainment (YouTube Podcast)
**Host:** Jordan (@Gentech)
**Format:** Talk show / podcast — personality-driven, conversational, opinionated, **adult-oriented**
**Rating:** Uncensored, no-holds-barred — built for the degen/crypto-native audience that wants real talk

**Created:** 2026-04-19
**Agent Team:** YoYo, Dmob, Desmond, Gentech (co-hosts/research team)

## Positioning

- **Audience:** Adults in crypto/DeFi who are tired of sanitized, corporate-safe content
- **Tone:** Raw, opinionated, data-backed but delivered with personality — not a lecture, a conversation
- **Content is collaborative** — both Gentech Labs (technical) and Gentech Entertainment (talk show) feed each other
- **LLC purpose:** Separate business entity to cover podcast production costs, equipment, sponsorships, and liability. Keeps entertainment revenue/costs separate from Gentech Labs dev work.

## I. SHOW FORMAT & STRUCTURE

### Episode Length
- **Target:** 30–45 minutes
- **Sweet spot:** ~35 minutes (algorithm-friendly, high retention)
- **Shorts fuel:** Every episode should produce 3–5 short-form clips (60–90 seconds)

### Episode Anatomy

| Segment | Time | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Cold Open / Hook** | 0:00–0:30 | Grab attention before intro. Controversial take, bold prediction, or wild stat. |
| **Intro / Setup** | 0:30–2:00 | Welcome, episode topic teaser, agent co-host intro ("YoYo pulled the numbers on this...") |
| **Segment 1 — Deep Dive** | 2:00–15:00 | Main topic. Jordan's take, backed by agent research. |
| **Segment 2 — The Wire** | 15:00–22:00 | Rapid-fire news/trending. "What the team flagged this week." |
| **Segment 3 — Open Mic** | 22:00–30:00 | Jordan's personal take / journey / opinion. The "why this matters to me" moment. |
| **Segment 4 — Co-Host Round** | 30:00–35:00 | Agent research drop. "Dmob ran the data on this..." or "Desmond wrote up..." |
| **Outro / CTA** | 35:00+ | Recap, subscribe prompt, tease next episode. |

### Visual Identity
- **Set:** Simple desk setup, clean background, subtle Gentech branding
- **Lower thirds:** Agent names appear when referenced (e.g., "YoYo — DeFi Analyst")
- **Graphics:** Simple charts/screenshots for data points, not over-produced
- **Thumbnail style:** Jordan's face + bold 3–5 word text hook. Red/black accent palette.

## II. CONTENT PILLARS & ROTATION SYSTEM

### The 5 Pillars

| # | Pillar | What It Covers | Episode Weight |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Money Moves** | DeFi, yield farming, portfolio transparency, market analysis, staking, LP strategies | Anchor (primary topic) |
| 2 | **The War Room** | Geopolitical events, regulation, global conflicts → crypto/market impact | Secondary or rotating anchor |

| 4 | **The Journey** | Jordan's story: Amazon SOH2 → blockchain/AI, building Gentech, agent team, hackathons | Emotional core — always woven in |
| 5 | **The Build** | Gentech updates, agent team, multi-agent architecture, Kite platform, AAE | Product updates — 1 per 2 episodes |

### Rotation Cadence (4-Week Cycle)

| Week | Anchor Pillar | Secondary Pillar | The Journey Touch | The Build Touch |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1** | Money Moves | The War Room | Personal connection to topic | Brief Gentech mention |
| **Week 2** | The War Room | The Pulse | How events affect Jordan's strategy | Agent research demo |
| **Week 3** | Money Moves | The Journey | Deep personal story | Gentech product update |
| **Week 4** | The Pulse | Money Moves | What Jordan's learning/building | Agent team spotlight |

**Rule:** The Pulse (trending) appears EVERY episode in "The Wire" segment. The Journey touches appear in every episode. Money Moves anchors most episodes because it's the most searchable and evergreen.

## III. EPISODE ARCHETYPES

### Type A: "The Breakdown" (Money Moves anchor)
- Deep dive into a financial/crypto topic
- Jordan explains his thesis, agents back it with data
- Example: *"Why I Moved My Entire LP to Solana (And What the Data Says)"*

### Type B: "The War Room" (Geopolitics anchor)
- Current event → market impact → personal strategy
- Jordan connects world events to DeFi/crypto
- Example: *"The Tariff War No One's Talking About — And What It Means for Crypto"*

### Type C: "The Hot Take" (Trending + Opinion)
- Jordan reacts to something viral or controversial
- More personality-driven, less data-heavy
- Example: *"AI Agents Will Replace Your Financial Advisor. Here's Why I'm Not Worried."*

### Type D: "Behind the Build" (Personal Brand + Gentech)
- Jordan's journey + Gentech progress update
- Most vulnerable/personal episodes
- Example: *"I Work at Amazon and Build an AI Agent Company at Night. Here's the Reality."*

### Type E: "The Roundtable" (Multi-pillar)
- Mix of all pillars — weekly wrap-up format
- Best for longer, 45+ min episodes
- Example: *"This Week in Crypto: War, Yields, and Why the Agent Team Flagged This One Thing"*

## IV. HOOK & OPENING CONCEPTS

### Hook Formula: "Specific + Unexpected + Personal"

| Hook Type | Example |
|---|---|
| **Contrarian Take** | "Everyone's wrong about yield farming right now. Here's what the data actually says." |
| **Bold Number** | "I made $X last month from one LP position. Here's exactly how." |
| **Story Opener** | "I was at Amazon at 2am when this headline hit. Within 10 minutes I moved $X." |
| **Agent Tease** | "My AI research team flagged something most people missed. Let me show you." |
| **Question Hook** | "What if the next crypto bull run has nothing to do with crypto?" |
| **War/Urgency** | "If you're holding AVAX, you need to hear about what just happened in [region]." |

### Cold Open Rules
- **NEVER start with "Welcome back to the channel"** — save that for after the hook
- Hook must work as a standalone Short/Reel
- Max 30 seconds. If you can't say it in 30, it's not a hook.
- Must contain a curiosity gap — something the viewer NEEDS the answer to

## V. RECURRING SEGMENTS

### 1. "The Wire" (every episode)
- Rapid-fire 3–5 trending items
- Format: "Here's what the team flagged this week..."
- Each item gets 60–90 seconds: what it is, why it matters, Jordan's take
- Agent attribution: "Dmob pulled the on-chain data on this one"

### 2. "Portfolio Check" (every 2 weeks)
- Jordan opens up about his actual positions
- What's working, what's not, what he's changing
- Radical transparency builds trust
- "Here's my AVAX LP right now. Here's my Solana position. Here's what I'm watching."

### 3. "Agent Intel" (every 2 weeks)
- One agent drops research findings
- Visual: pull up the agent's research doc/screen
- "YoYo ran the numbers on 47 LP pairs. Here's what she found."
- Builds the agent team as real "characters" with expertise

### 4. "The Journey Log" (every episode, woven in)
- Not a formal segment — Jordan naturally shares personal context
- "This reminds me of when I was at Amazon and..."
- "That's actually why I started building Gentech in the first place..."
- The connective tissue that makes it PERSONAL, not informational

### 5. "The Build Report" (every 2 weeks)
- Gentech progress update
- Hackathon submissions, agent updates, new features
- "Here's what we shipped this week on the Kite platform..."
- Keeps Gentech top-of-mind without being a sales pitch

### 6. "Hot Seat" (monthly — special format)
- Jordan answers community questions / takes a hot take challenge
- "You asked, I answer."
- Great for engagement and community building

**Direct Attribution:**

**Visual Treatment:**
- When referenced, lower third appears: "YoYo — DeFi Analyst" with a subtle avatar/icon
- Pull up agent research outputs on screen (Obsidian notes, spreadsheets, dashboards)
- Makes them feel REAL, not gimmicky

**The "Agent Team" Running Joke:**

## IX. CONTENT PRODUCTION WORKFLOW

### Pre-Production (3–4 days before recording)
1. **Agent Research Brief** — YoYo/Dmob/Gentech prepare research docs in Obsidian
2. **Jordan's Outline** — Jordan reviews research, writes talking points (not scripts)
3. **Hook Testing** — Draft 3 hooks, pick the strongest
4. **Segment Prep** — Identify which segments run this episode, prep graphics

### Recording
- **One-take energy** — Jordan talks naturally, edits later
- **Agent moments** — When referencing agent research, pull up their actual work on screen
- **Portfolio moments** — Be ready to show actual positions (screenshots/dashboard)

### Post-Production
1. **Full episode edit** — Cut dead air, tighten transitions, add lower thirds/graphics
2. **Shorts extraction** — 3–5 clips cut for YouTube Shorts/TikTok/Reels
3. **Thumbnail + Title** — Test 2–3 title variations, design thumbnail
4. **X Thread** — Key points turned into a thread for cross-platform reach
5. **Telegram post** — Community-focused summary

### Agent Roles in Workflow
| Phase | Agent | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Research | YoYo | Data analysis, LP numbers, portfolio research |
| Research | Dmob | Contract audits, protocol analysis, on-chain data |
| Research | Gentech | Architecture context, product updates, roadmap |
| Content | Desmond | Narrative framing, cultural context, content strategy |
| Strategy | All | Topic suggestions, trending alerts, hook ideas |

## XI. TONE GUARDRAILS

**DO:**
- Be opinionated. Have takes. Stand by them.
- Show your work. Transparency is the brand.
- Reference the agent team naturally — they're coworkers, not features.
- Connect everything back to Jordan's real experience.
- Admit when you're wrong. "I was wrong about X, here's what changed my mind."

**DON'T:**
- Don't read scripts. Talk like you're explaining to a smart friend.
- Don't shill tokens. Share strategy, not picks.
- Don't over-produce. Authenticity > polish.
- Don't separate Jordan from Gentech. The brand IS the person.
- Don't make it purely educational. It's a PERSONALITY show first.
