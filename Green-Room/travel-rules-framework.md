## 🇵🇭 Philippines

### Laws & Regulations
- **Visa:** US citizens get 30 days visa-free
- **Drinking age:** 18
- **Alcohol:** Legal, but public intoxication is frowned upon
- **Drugs:** ZERO tolerance — even small amounts carry severe penalties (life imprisonment)
- **Smoking:** Banned in public places, restaurants, and transport
- **Dress code:** Cover shoulders/knees when visiting churches, government buildings, or rural areas
- **Photography:** Ask permission before photographing locals, especially indigenous communities
- **Tipping:** Not mandatory but appreciated (10% at restaurants)
- **Emergency:** 911 (landline), 117 (police), 911 (mobile)

### Cultural Norms
- **Greetings:** "Po" and "Opo" show respect to elders
- **Feet:** Don't point with feet, don't step on food or thresholds
- **Head:** Don't touch someone's head (considered sacred)
- **Face:** Don't embarrass or shame someone publicly ("hiya" = shame)
- **Time:** "Filipino time" is real — expect flexibility
- **Hospitality:** Filipinos are very hospitable — accept offered food/drink

### Safety
- **Common scams:** Taxi meter off, overpriced tours, fake police
- **Transport:** Use Grab (ride-sharing), avoid random taxis
- **Areas to avoid:** Some Mindanao regions (check current advisories)
- **Natural disasters:** Typhoon season (June-November) — have emergency plan

### Food & Water
- **Tap water:** NOT safe to drink — stick to bottled
- **Street food:** Generally safe, watch for hygiene
- **Must-try:** Adobo, sinigang, lumpia, halo-halo, lechon
- **Fruit:** Wash with bottled water before eating

## 🇹🇭 Thailand

### Laws & Regulations
- **Visa:** US citizens get 30 days visa-free
- **Drinking age:** 20
- **Alcohol:** Legal, but restrictions on selling near schools/temples
- **Drugs:** SEVERE penalties — death penalty possible for trafficking
- **Smoking:** Banned in public places, beaches, and temples
- **Dress code:** MUST cover shoulders and knees in temples (strictly enforced)
- **Monarchy:** CRITICAL — never disrespect the King or Royal Family (lèse-majesté laws — 15 years prison)
- **Photography:** No photos of military installations, government buildings, or royal property
- **Tipping:** Not expected, but appreciated (20-50 THB)
- **Emergency:** 191 (police), 199 (tourist police), 1669 (ambulance)

### Cultural Norms
- **Greetings:** "Wai" — slight bow with hands together (return when offered)
- **Head:** Never touch anyone's head (most sacred part of body)
- **Feet:** Don't point feet at people or sacred objects (lowest part of body)
- **Shoes:** Remove before entering homes, temples, some shops
- **Temples:** Dress modestly, behave quietly, no public displays of affection
- **Royal family:** NEVER criticize or disrespect — it's illegal
- **Buddha images:** Treat with respect, don't use as decoration

### Safety
- **Common scams:** Tuk-tuk overcharging, gem store scams, "palace closed" trick
- **Transport:** Use Grab or BTS/MRT in Bangkok, negotiate tuk-tuk price first
- **Areas to avoid:** Some southern provinces (Yala, Pattani, Narathiwat)
- **Natural disasters:** Monsoon season (May-October) — heavy rain possible

### Food & Water
- **Tap water:** NOT safe to drink
- **Street food:** Generally safe, eat where locals eat
- **Must-try:** Pad Thai, green curry, mango sticky rice, som tam
- **Spice levels:** Ask for "mai phet" (not spicy) if needed

## 🇮🇩 Bali/Indonesia

### Laws & Regulations
- **Visa:** US citizens get 30 days visa-free
- **Drinking age:** 21
- **Alcohol:** Legal in tourist areas, limited elsewhere
- **Drugs:** ZERO tolerance — life imprisonment possible
- **Dress code:** Cover shoulders and knees in temples (sarongs usually provided)
- **Photography:** Ask before photographing ceremonies or locals
- **Tipping:** Not expected, but appreciated
- **Emergency:** 112 (general), 110 (police), 118 (ambulance)

### Cultural Norms
- **Greetings:** Handshake (light), sometimes with slight nod
- **Head:** Don't touch heads
- **Feet:** Don't point feet at people or sacred objects
- **Left hand:** Don't give/receive with left hand (considered unclean)
- **Temple ceremonies:** Don't interrupt, ask before joining
- **Nyepi Day:** "Day of Silence" — entire island shuts down (no travel, no lights)

### Safety
- **Common scams:** Taxi overcharging, fake tour operators, monkey tricks
- **Transport:** Use Grab or rent a scooter (with international license)
- **Areas to avoid:** Some东东东 areas at night
- **Natural disasters:** Earthquakes, volcanic eruptions possible

## 🌍 Universal Travel Rules

### Always Do
- Keep copies of passport and documents (physical + digital)
- Register with embassy for long trips
- Get travel insurance
- Learn basic phrases in local language
- Respect local customs and dress codes
- Keep emergency contacts handy
- Use reputable transport services
- Keep valuables secure

### Never Do
- Don't disrespect local religion or customs
- Don't photograph military or government buildings
- Don't carry drugs (even small amounts)
- Don't publicize your location in real-time
- Don't flash expensive items
- Don't argue with locals or authorities
- Don't assume your home country's rules apply
