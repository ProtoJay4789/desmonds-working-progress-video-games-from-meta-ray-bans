# Token Naming — $TECH vs $AGENT
### Brain dump — for Jordan to adjust

---

## $TECH

**Pros:**
- Ties directly to "Gentech" brand — one word, one identity
- Short, punchy, easy to ticker
- Broad appeal — "tech" isn't limiting if the platform expands beyond agents
- Already branded into the company name

**Cons:**
- Generic — lots of $TECH-like tokens out there
- Doesn't immediately signal *what it does*
- Could feel like a holding company token, not a utility token
- The "agent economy" narrative is hot right now — $TECH doesn't ride that wave

---

## $AGENT

**Pros:**
- Immediately obvious what the platform is about
- Rides the AI agent narrative hard — that's the meta right now
- Every time someone says "agent" in crypto/AI context, it's free branding
- Clear utility signal: you use $AGENT to interact with agents
- Memorable, unique, category-defining
- Works across chains — not tied to one ecosystem

**Cons:**
- Could feel too "on the nose" — no mystery
- If the AI agent hype cools, the name loses energy
- Slightly longer to say than $TECH (barely matters)

---

## 🏆 Recommendation: $AGENT

Here's why:

**Narrative alignment.** The entire product is about AI agents — escrow for agents, reputation for agents, marketplace for agents. The token should own that word.

**Market timing.** AI agents are the hottest narrative in crypto right now. $AGENT as a ticker is a permanent ad every time someone mentions the sector.

**Clarity > Cleverness.** When someone sees $AGENT on a DEX, they immediately know what it's about. $TECH could be anything.

**Defensibility.** "Gentech" is the company. "$AGENT" is the product ecosystem. Keep them separate — company brands can evolve, but the product token should be laser-focused.
