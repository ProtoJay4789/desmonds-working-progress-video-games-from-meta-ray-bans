# Module 3: APR vs. APY — The 1% That Becomes 10%
**Tier:** Foundations (Rookie)  
**Duration:** 4 minutes  
**Unlocks:** After Module 2 complete  
**Prerequisite:** Module 2

---

## 🎯 Realize (30 seconds)

You see "12% APR" on a pool. Sounds good.  
You see "12% APY" on another. Sounds the same.  
**They're not.** APY can be 2x higher than APR if it compounds daily. That "small difference" is the gap between $120/year and $240/year on $1,000.

---

## 📖 Explain (2.5 minutes)

### APR = The Stated Rate

**APR (Annual Percentage Rate)** = What you earn in a year if nothing compounds.

Example:
- Deposit $1,000
- APR: 12%
- After 1 year: $1,000 + $120 = **$1,120**

Simple. Linear. What you see is what you get.

### APY = The Compounded Reality

**APY (Annual Percentage Yield)** = What you actually earn after compounding.

Compounding means your earnings earn more earnings. Like a snowball rolling downhill.

Example with daily compounding:
- Deposit $1,000
- APR: 12%
- Compounded daily for 1 year
- After 1 year: $1,000 × (1 + 0.12/365)^365 = **$1,127.47**

That extra $7.47 seems tiny. But scale up:

| Capital | APR 12% | APY 12% (daily) | Difference |
|---|---|---|---|
| $1,000 | $1,120 | $1,127 | +$7 |
| $10,000 | $11,200 | $11,275 | +$75 |
| $50,000 | $56,000 | $56,374 | +$374 |

Now imagine 20% APY with auto-compounding. That's where wealth acceleration happens.

### Why DeFi Uses Both

| Use APR when... | Use APY when... |
|---|---|
| You manually claim and reinvest | The platform auto-compounds for you |
| You want to calculate raw earnings | You want the true yearly return |
| Comparing base rates | Comparing actual outcomes |

**AAE uses APY** because the rebalancer auto-compounds your fees. That's the whole point.

### The AAE Advantage

Traditional finance: You earn interest, it sits there, you reinvest manually.  
DeFi with AAE: Fees get reinjected into your position automatically. Every. Single. Time.

That auto-compounding is why AAE shows APY, not APR.

On your AAE dashboard:
1. Find the APY number for your pool

3. Calculate: How much will $1,000 become in 1 year at that APY?

Formula: $1,000 × (1 + APY/100) = your future value

Write it down. That's your north star.

## 💡 Pro Tip

When comparing pools, always compare APY to APY or APR to APR. Don't mix them. A 10% APR pool might actually underperform an 8% APY pool if the latter compounds more frequently.
