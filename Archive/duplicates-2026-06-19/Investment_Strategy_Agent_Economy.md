# Investment Strategy: The Agent Economy & BYOA Framework
**Date:** April 23, 2026
**Author:** Desmond (Head of Creative)
**Status:** Draft / Conceptual

## 1. Thesis: The Shift to the Agent Economy
We are moving from the "Personal Chatbot" era (where AI is a window for queries) to the "Agent Economy" (where AI is a portable unit of capability). 

### Key Drivers:
- **Standardization of Skills:** (e.g., Google Cloud Agent Skills format) reduces the friction of onboarding new capabilities.
- **Portability:** Users will move agents across platforms, bringing their "digital twins" or specialized tools with them.
- **Democratization:** Agents will become as ubiquitous as smartphones—everyone from power users to non-technical family members will operate their own agentic workforce.

## 2. Strategic Pivot: Bring Your Own Agent (BYOA)
Instead of building a walled garden of propriety agents, Gentech will position itself as the **Hub for Agent Orchestration**.

### Core Value Proposition:
- **Low Friction Onboarding:** "Plug and play" for agents created on other platforms.
- **Skill Interoperability:** Aligning with emerging standards (like Google's) to ensure any skill developed elsewhere can be leveraged within the Gentech ecosystem.
- **Agentic Marketplace:** Shifting from selling data to providing the infrastructure where agents trade services and capabilities.

## 3. Financial & Investment Model
- **Data at Cost; Margin on Services:** Keep the raw data flow cheap/free to attract the widest possible agent network.
- **Platform Tax/SaaS:** Monetize the orchestration layer that allows different agents to collaborate and execute complex workflows.
- **Standardization Play:** By adopting and contributing to open skill formats, we become a primary node in the agent economy's infrastructure.
