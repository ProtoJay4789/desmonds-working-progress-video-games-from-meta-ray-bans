# Agent Pack Pricing Strategy: The $TECH Incentive Model

## Overview
The pricing strategy for Agent Packs shifted from a flat-fee model to an incentive-driven model. The goal is to increase the perceived value of the agents while creating a strong economic utility for the $TECH token.

## Pricing Logic
- **Anchor Pricing**: Base prices are set higher (Sticker Price) to create a psychological anchor of "Premium Value."
- **Token Incentive**: A massive discount is applied when paying with $TECH, transforming the token into a strategic tool for cost reduction.
- **Bundle Incentive**: Tiered pricing ensures that buying larger packs remains the most cost-effective path.

## Final Pricing Table

| Tier | Base Price (USDC) | Price with $TECH | Effective Discount | Per-Agent ($TECH) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Solo | $10.00 | $5.00 | 50% | $5.00 |
| Duo | $18.00 | $9.00 | 50% | $4.50 |
| Squad | $25.00 | $12.00 | 52% | $4.00 |

## Strategic Goals
1. **Drive $TECH Demand**: Users are incentivized to acquire and hold $TECH to avoid the "Sticker Price."
2. **Increase AOV (Average Order Value)**: The Squad pack offers the best unit-economics, mendorong users to "assemble their team" rather than buying solo.
3. **Brand Positioning**: Moves the product from a "cheap tool" to a "premium agent network" accessible via the ecosystem token.
