## 2026-06-14 — Bid-Ask Rebalance & Sunday Volatility

**Time:** ~4:30 PM EDT
**Position:** AVAX-USDC on LFJ (Trader Joe)
**Action:** Rebalanced from Convergent (6.43–6.59) → Bid-Ask (6.30–6.55)

### Market Context
- AVAX at $6.45, down ~3.8% in 24h
- Four-year cycle over — entering long-term consolidation
- Expecting 6 months of sideways/depressed action before next leg up
- Sunday always tricky — lower liquidity, whipsaw potential

### Strategy Rationale
- **Bid-Ask shape chosen deliberately:** Liquidity concentrated at range edges (6.30 and 6.55)
- When price is in the center (like now at $6.45), fees are thin — that's by design
- Fees spike when price swings toward the edges — we're collecting premium on volatility
- Bid-ask benefits from the exact kind of dip/rip action Sunday tends to produce
- If AVAX dips toward $6.30, we earn more. If it rips toward $6.55, we earn more.
- Center is the "dead zone" — but that's where we are now, so patience is key

### Process Notes
- This is the evolution from static positions to active strategy
- Dashboard now shows shape-aware efficiency (edge zone vs center zone)
- Bug tracker established — every issue gets logged and fixed
- We're building the test suite as we go — every bug makes the next version cleaner

### Risk Assessment
- Position size: $44.74 (small, learning position)
- If AVAX breaks below $6.30 → out of range, need rebalance
- If AVAX breaks above $6.55 → out of range, need rebalance
- Sunday dip could push us toward lower edge — watching closely

### Key Learning
> "Sunday is always tricky. The bid-ask will just take up some of this dip."

This is the insight: instead of fighting volatility, we're positioning to **profit from it**. The bid-ask shape is a volatility strategy, not a trend strategy. In consolidation (which we're entering), this is the right play.
