# Module 1: What Is Liquidity Providing?
**Tier:** Foundations (Rookie)  
**Duration:** 5 minutes  
**Unlocks:** After first deposit  
**Prerequisite:** None

---

## 🎯 Realize (30 seconds)

You know how a currency exchange booth at the airport needs both dollars *and* euros to swap between them?  
**You are that booth.**  
Liquidity providing = you deposit two tokens into a pool so traders can swap between them. In return, you earn a cut of every trade.

---

## 📖 Explain (3 minutes)

### The Airport Booth Analogy

Imagine you run a small exchange booth:
- You put **$100 USD** and **€100 EUR** in your drawer
- A tourist swaps $10 → €9. You keep a small fee ($0.03)
- Another tourist swaps €20 → $22. You keep another fee ($0.06)
- At the end of the day, you've earned fees just for *having* money in the drawer

**That's liquidity providing.** Only instead of USD/EUR, it's ETH/USDC. Or AVAX/USDC. Or any token pair.

### Why Traders Need You

Traders want to swap Token A → Token B instantly. They don't want to wait for a specific person to show up. The liquidity pool is like a vending machine — always on, always stocked, always ready.

**Your deposit = the stock in the machine.**

### What You Actually Earn

Every swap has a **0.3% fee** (varies by platform). That fee gets split among all liquidity providers proportional to their share of the pool.

Example:
- Pool has $100,000 total
- You deposited $1,000 → you own **1%** of the pool
- Today, $50,000 in trades happened → $150 in fees generated
- Your cut: **1% of $150 = $1.50**

That $1.50 is yours. It compounds automatically.

### The Catch (Preview)

There's one risk called "impermanent loss" — but don't worry about it yet. Module 2 covers it in plain English. For now, just know: **the fees usually outweigh the risk** if you pick the right pool and time frame.
