## ⚠️ CRACKDOWN STATUS (June 2026)

Thailand is actively tightening rules for tourists. Multiple changes are in effect or coming soon.

### What Changed (May 19, 2026 — Cabinet Approved)

| Rule | Before | Now |
|------|--------|-----|
| **Visa-exempt stay** | 60 days | **30 days** |
| **Eligible countries** | 93 | **54** |
| **Visa on Arrival** | 31 countries | **4 only** (Azerbaijan, Belarus, Serbia, India) |
| **Multiple exemptions** | Overlapping OK | **Single privilege per country** |

**Status:** Approved in principle, NOT yet in effect. Takes effect 15 days after Government Gazette publication. Monitor closely.

**What this means for Jordan (US passport):** US is still on the 30-day visa-exempt list. But the 60-day window is gone. Plan for 30 days max per entry.

### Proof of Funds Requirement

**Rule:** Tourists must possess adequate cash of **20,000 Baht per person** (~$550 USD) or 40,000 Baht per family.

**Enforcement:** Immigration randomly pulls people out of line to check. They want to see **CASH**, not bank statements. Bring physical Thai Baht.

**Source:** Thai Embassy official requirement + Tim Travel Taste video (June 2026)

### Overstay Penalties

| Penalty | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Fine** | 500 Baht/day, capped at 20,000 Baht (~$550) |
| **Max fine** | 20,000 Baht |
| **Jail** | Up to 2 years (statutory) |
| **Re-entry ban** | 1–10 years depending on overstay length |
| **Arrested while overstaying** | Mandatory deportation + 5-10 year ban |

### Unruly Tourist Crackdown (May 2026)

PM Anutin Charnvirakul ordered nationwide enforcement:
- **Zero tolerance** in Phuket — maximum penalties, immediate visa revocation for serious offenses
- **No driving without valid license** — court appearance without exception
- **Cultural respect enforced** — no bullying locals, no disrespectful behavior
- **Nominee businesses investigated** — foreigners using tourist visas to work

### Mandatory Health Insurance (Proposed)

Thailand is considering **mandatory health insurance** for all foreign visitors before entry. Not yet implemented but being reviewed.

**Action:** Get travel insurance anyway. Covers medical emergencies + shows proof if needed.

## 📋 PRE-TRIP CHECKLIST

### Documents
- [ ] Passport valid 6+ months beyond Nov 2026
- [ ] Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC) — required since May 2025, replaces TM6 form
- [ ] Proof of onward travel (return ticket)
- [ ] Hotel booking confirmation
- [ ] Travel health insurance (get it regardless of mandate)
- [ ] **20,000 Baht cash** (~$550) — for proof of funds check

### Money
- [ ] Bring 20,000 Baht cash per person (USD → THB at airport or SuperRich)
- [ ] Debit card for ATM withdrawals (fee ~220 Baht per withdrawal)
- [ ] Credit card for hotels/emergencies
- [ ] Keep cash visible for immigration check — don't hide it

### Behavior Rules (Crackdown-Proof)
- [ ] **No vaping** — 100% illegal, fine up to 30,000 Baht
- [ ] **Weed** — legal to buy, never smoke in public (25,000 Baht fine)
- [ ] **Scooter** — need International Driving Permit + motorcycle license or no insurance coverage
- [ ] **Temples** — cover knees and shoulders
- [ ] **National anthem** — 8 AM and 6 PM, stop and stand still
- [ ] **Royal family** — zero tolerance for jokes or insults (lèse-majesté)
- [ ] **Don't touch anyone's head** — sacred in Thai culture
- [ ] **Don't point feet at Buddha statues or people**

### Land Border Rules
- Max **2 entries per calendar year** via land/sea border (visa exemption)
- Air entries don't count toward this limit
- Malaysia, Brunei, Indonesia, Singapore exempt from this limit

## 🇵🇭 PHILIPPINES — 2026 RULES

**Good news: Philippines is NOT cracking down like Thailand.**

### US Passport (Jordan)
- **30 days visa-free** — unchanged, 150+ nations eligible
- **Extendable** via Bureau of Immigration (up to 36 months total)
- **eTravel registration** required (health/border form, NOT a visa)

### Key Differences from Thailand
| Rule | Thailand | Philippines |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Visa-free stay | 30 days (was 60) | **30 days (unchanged)** |
| Proof of funds | 20,000 Baht cash enforced | Required but rarely checked |
| Crackdown | Zero tolerance, deportation | No major crackdown |
| Health insurance | May become mandatory | Not required |
| e-visa | Traditional embassy | e-visa expanding (optional) |

### Philippines Entry Requirements
- Valid passport (6+ months beyond stay)
- Return/onward ticket
- Proof of accommodation
- Proof of sufficient funds (bank statement OK, cash preferred)
- **eTravel registration** — mandatory, do online before arrival

### Philippines Extensions
- **30 days** visa-free → extend 29 days more ( Php 3,030 / ~$55)
- Can extend up to **36 months** total with Bureau of Immigration
- Fine for overstaying: Php 500/month

### Philippines Strategy for September Trip
1. **Enter visa-free** — 30 days, no paperwork
2. **Extend at Bureau of Immigration** if staying longer — cheap and easy
3. **Complete eTravel** online before departure
4. **Bring proof of funds** — just in case (less enforced than Thailand)
5. **No health insurance required** — but get it anyway (same providers work)
